# Report Quality Standard

## Purpose

This document defines the quality standard for every Brand Strategy Report generated by Project Origin.

The objective is simple:

> Every report should provide more strategic value than a generic ChatGPT response.

Project Origin is not judged by the quality of its code.

It is judged by the quality of its reports.

---

# Core Principle

A report should never feel AI-generated.

A report should feel like it was written by an experienced brand strategist.

---

# Quality Pillars

Every report must satisfy five pillars.

## 1. Strategic Depth

Questions:

* Does the report explain WHY?
* Does it identify founder intent?
* Does it reveal insights beyond the interview?

Score:

1 ~ 5

---

## 2. Evidence-Based Reasoning

Questions:

* Is every conclusion supported by interview data?
* Are assumptions clearly separated from facts?
* Does the report avoid hallucination?

Score:

1 ~ 5

---

## 3. Originality

Questions:

* Could ChatGPT generate this report with a simple prompt?
* Does the report contain unique strategic thinking?
* Does it avoid generic business language?

Score:

1 ~ 5

---

## 4. Actionability

Questions:

* Can the founder make decisions immediately?
* Does the report provide concrete recommendations?
* Are next actions clearly explained?

Score:

1 ~ 5

---

## 5. Professional Presentation

Questions:

* Is the report easy to read?
* Is the structure logical?
* Does it resemble a consulting report?

Score:

1 ~ 5

---

# Section Standards

## Executive Summary

Requirements

* Clearly summarize the business.
* Explain founder intent.
* Introduce strategic direction.

Fail if:

* Only repeats interview answers.

---

## Founder Insights

Requirements

* Reveal motivations behind the founder.
* Explain hidden assumptions.
* Highlight strengths and risks.

Fail if:

* Simply paraphrases the interview.

---

## Brand Identity

Requirements

* Define the brand in one clear sentence.
* Differentiate from competitors.
* Reflect founder philosophy.

---

## Mission Statement

Requirements

* One sentence.
* Clear.
* Memorable.
* Action-oriented.

---

## Vision Statement

Requirements

* Long-term.
* Inspiring.
* Ambitious.
* Realistic.

---

## Core Values

Requirements

* 3–5 values.
* Each value must include a short explanation.
* Values must come from interview evidence.

---

## Positioning

Requirements

Must explain:

* Target customer
* Alternative
* Differentiation
* Reason to choose

---

## Brand Personality

Requirements

* Select one primary archetype.
* Explain why.
* Reference interview evidence.

---

## Naming Strategy

Requirements

Explain:

* Naming philosophy
* Tone
* Emotional direction
* Linguistic direction

---

## Name Recommendations

Exactly five candidates.

Each candidate must include:

* Meaning
* Strategic Fit
* Strengths
* Weaknesses
* Score (0–10)
* Reason for score

---

## Final Recommendation

Requirements

Choose one recommendation.

Explain:

* Why it is the best.
* Long-term scalability.
* Strategic alignment.

---

# Automatic Failure Conditions

A report automatically fails if:

* Generic business buzzwords dominate.
* Founder interview is merely paraphrased.
* Unsupported assumptions are presented as facts.
* Recommendations lack reasoning.
* Every founder receives essentially the same report.

---

# Quality Goal

Target average score:

22 / 25 or higher.

Only reports meeting this standard should be delivered to users.
